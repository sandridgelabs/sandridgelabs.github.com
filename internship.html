Summer 2011 Web Development Internship

Overview

I am an independent web developer, who is looking for a summer intern to assist on web development this summer. I have a list of various projects I'd like to work on, so there would be some flexibility for you to choose a project that interests you most. There may also be the possibility of collaborating on a new project proposed by you. The primary tools I use are Ruby, Rails, MySQL, CoffeeScript.

I am only accepting one intern this summer. I need help in all aspects of web development, including programming, graphic design, and system administration. If any (or all) of these areas interest you, please apply.

About Me

I have been programming computers since I was in high school (about 25 years), with my first paid programming job coming my freshman year of college. I have experience in a variety of languages and environments, but now prefer working on Macs & Linux, using primarily Ruby. I have 5 years of Ruby experience. I have an interest in mentoring, and have mentored others in the past.

Length / Location

The duration of the internship is flexible, with a goal of it lasting about 6-12 weeks. There may be an opportunity to continue into the fall depending on the situation. The number of hours a week is also flexible, within the range of 15-30 hours per week. Work will be completed on site in Arlington, VA, and is a 10-15 minute walk from the Clarendon Metro station.

Qualifications

At a minimum, you must have moderate computer skills with Mac OS (sorry, no Windows here). For programmers, you should also have experience in at least one computer programming language. For system administration, you need to have experience on a unix/linux command line. You must be fluent in English.

Compensation / Credit

Compensation will vary depending on level of the skills/experience of the intern, ranging from unpaid ($0/hr) to a maximum paid compensation of $15/hour. Course credit may also be possible. If interested in credit, please provide contact information to the individual or department of your school who is responsible for your school's internship program.

How to Apply

Students wishing to apply, should email with the following information:

  • a brief (one to two paragraphs) introduction to yourself, including your current level of education, and what you'd like to accomplish with the internship
  • Résumé (if you have one), otherwise a list of all relevant skills/experience
  • Recommendation, with contact information, preferably from a computer/mathematics/science teacher or previous employer
  • Additional Information: Programmers are encouraged to submit code samples (or links to a public code repository) if they have them; and graphic designers are encouraged to submit a design portfolio if they have one.
